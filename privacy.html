Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Overview

Via Clara ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020. This policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information.

Who we are

Via Clara is an execution transformation consulting practice operating across Australia and New Zealand. You can contact us at contact@viaclara.co.nz or +64 27 802 4143.

What information we collect

We may collect personal information when you:

  • Contact us via email or phone
  • Download resources from this website
  • Engage us for consulting services

The information we collect may include your name, email address, phone number, organisation name, and the content of any messages you send us.

How we use your information

  • To respond to your enquiries
  • To deliver consulting services you have engaged us for
  • To send you resources or information you have requested
  • To comply with legal obligations

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

How we store your information

Personal information is stored securely and retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law.

Your rights

Under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, you have the right to access personal information we hold about you, and to request correction of that information. To make a request, contact us at contact@viaclara.co.nz.

Cookies and analytics

This website uses Google Analytics to collect anonymised data about how visitors use the site, including pages visited and time spent. This data is aggregated and does not identify individual users. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect this information. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The current version will always be available at this URL.

Complaints

If you have a concern about how we have handled your personal information, please contact us in the first instance. You also have the right to make a complaint to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner at privacy.org.nz.
